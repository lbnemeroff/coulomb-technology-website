Integration Architecture

Three Protocols.
One DC Block.

The Series-DC communicates over Modbus TCP, CAN Bus 2.0B, and RS-485 simultaneously — so your inverter, EMS, and SCADA platform all speak to the battery natively, without gateways or translators.

01
Modbus TCP
Ethernet / IP Protocol

Industry-standard TCP/IP protocol for integration with SCADA systems, building management systems, and any IP-connected EMS or monitoring platform.

  • Real-time SOC, SOH, voltage, current, temperature
  • Cell-level monitoring via register map
  • Remote charge/discharge control
  • Alarm and fault status registers
  • Compatible with all major SCADA platforms
02
CAN Bus 2.0B
Automotive / Industrial Protocol

High-speed CAN Bus 2.0B for direct integration with inverters and power conversion systems that use automotive-grade communication — common in EV chargers and industrial UPS systems.

  • Up to 1 Mbit/s data rate
  • Direct inverter-to-BMS communication
  • Standard message frames for SOC and limits
  • Charge/discharge current limit broadcast
  • Compatible with SMA, SolarEdge, Victron, and more
03
RS-485
Serial / Legacy Protocol

RS-485 serial interface for integration with legacy EMS platforms, building automation systems, and industrial controllers that don't support Ethernet or CAN.

  • Multi-drop bus — up to 32 devices per segment
  • Long-distance runs up to 1,200 meters
  • Noise-immune differential signaling
  • Compatible with legacy BMS and SCADA
  • Modbus RTU over RS-485 supported
