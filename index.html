The Technology Advantage

Why Sodium-Ion?

Lithium-ion was designed for consumer electronics. Sodium-ion was engineered for the demands of commercial and industrial infrastructure.

The Problem with Lithium-Ion

Safety Risks

Thermal runaway incidents complicate permitting, increase insurance costs, and create liability exposure for property owners and operators.

Temperature Limitations

Performance degrades significantly in extreme heat or cold, reducing reliability in outdoor and industrial environments.

Supply Chain Exposure

Concentrated global sourcing of lithium, cobalt, and nickel creates procurement uncertainty and geopolitical risk.

The Sodium-Ion Advantage

Enhanced Safety

Reduced thermal propagation vs. lithium-ion enables safer installations in occupied buildings, warehouses, and urban environments.

Extreme Temperature Range

−40°C to +55°C operation with minimal derating. Performs reliably in cold storage, outdoor installations, and desert climates.

Domestic Supply Chain

Sodium is abundant and domestically available. U.S. manufacturing pathway reduces geopolitical risk and qualifies for domestic content incentives.

Lower Total Cost of Ownership

14% better economics over 15 years vs. lithium-ion, with 20-year design life, 6,000+ cycle life, and lower insurance and permitting costs.

Sodium is 1,000× more abundant than lithium in the Earth's crust — making Coulomb's supply chain fundamentally more resilient than any lithium-based technology.
